# Functional Requirements

All requirements are classified under the following four-level priority scale:

| Level | Name     | Description                                                                                          | Impact if Missing                                          |
|-------|----------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------|
| P0    | Critical | Launch blocker. Without this requirement the system cannot operate safely or coherently.             | System inoperable or insecure.                             |
| P1    | High     | Essential for the core value proposition. Must be present in v1.0 even if temporary workarounds exist. | Key process degraded, requires manual intervention.        |
| P2    | Medium   | Important for user experience or operational efficiency. Can be deferred to v1.1 without compromising the core. | Degraded user experience or additional manual processes.   |
| P3    | Low      | Desirable in the future. Adds differential value but is not necessary in early versions. Later roadmap. | No immediate operational impact.                           |

## 4.1 Authentication and Account Management

| ID    | Module / Feature              | Description and Acceptance Criteria                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             | Priority    |
|-------|-------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------|
| RF-01 | Auth – Student Registration   | A student can create their account on the platform by providing the required data. If the student is a minor, they must register their tutor's data. **Acceptance Criteria:** The form validates mandatory fields: first name, last name, date of birth, identity number, document type, EPS. If the student is under 18 years old (calculated from date of birth), the form mandatorily requests the tutor's data (name, relationship, contact). The system sends a verification email before activating the account. Registration with an already existing identity number within the tenant is not allowed. The user is placed in a 'pending membership' state after successful registration. | P0 – Critical |
| RF-02 | Auth – Login / Logout         | Users can log in and out securely. **Acceptance Criteria:** Login accepts email address and password. After 5 failed attempts, the account is locked for 15 minutes. The session token expires after 8 hours of inactivity. Logout invalidates the token server-side. Each role (superadmin, admin, manager, professor, student) is redirected to their corresponding dashboard. | P0 – Critical |
| RF-03 | Auth – Password Recovery      | A user can recover their password through their registered email address. **Acceptance Criteria:** An email is sent with a password reset link valid for 30 minutes. The link can only be used once. The new password must have a minimum of 8 characters, at least one uppercase letter, one number, and one special character. | P1 – High   |
| RF-04 | Auth – Role and Permission Management | The system controls access to functionalities based on the authenticated user's role. **Acceptance Criteria:** Every route in the system verifies the user's role before rendering content. A user with the 'student' role cannot access manager, professor, or admin views. Permissions are managed by role and cannot be modified by the user. The superadmin can assign the administrator role to users within a tenant. The administrator can assign manager and professor roles within their tenant. | P0 – Critical |

## 4.2 Multitenant Management

| ID    | Module / Feature              | Description and Acceptance Criteria                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             | Priority    |
|-------|-------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------|
| RF-05 | Tenant – League Creation      | The superadmin can register new leagues (tenants) on the platform. **Acceptance Criteria:** The superadmin can create a tenant with: league name, sport/discipline, logo, and contact information. Each tenant has a unique subdomain or identifier in the URL. The data of one tenant is completely isolated from other tenants; no query returns cross-tenant data. The superadmin can deactivate a tenant, blocking access for all its users. | P0 – Critical |
| RF-06 | Tenant – Program Configuration | Each league can manage its own programs with independent configuration. **Acceptance Criteria:** A program belongs to a single tenant. The program has: name, modality (hours-based or classes-per-week), cost per modality, and an assigned manager. A tenant can have multiple active programs simultaneously. The configuration of one tenant's program does not affect other tenants. | P0 – Critical |

## 4.3 Program, Level, and Professor Management

| ID    | Module / Feature                        | Description and Acceptance Criteria                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             | Priority    |
|-------|-----------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------|
| RF-07 | Program – Level Management              | The manager can create and manage the levels within their program. **Acceptance Criteria:** Levels can be created with customizable names (beginner, intermediate, advanced, or others defined by the manager). Each level has an assigned professor. A level can be deactivated without deleting the associated student history. The order of levels is configurable (to define the promotion hierarchy). | P0 – Critical |
| RF-08 | Program – Professor Management          | The manager can register and assign professors to the program's levels. **Acceptance Criteria:** The manager can create a professor profile with first name, last name, email, and assign them to a level. A professor can be assigned to more than one level if the manager permits it. The system sends the professor an invitation email to activate their account. The manager can reassign or remove professors from a level. | P0 – Critical |
| RF-09 | Program – Class and Schedule Management | The manager can configure the classes available per level, with schedules and student capacity limits. **Acceptance Criteria:** A class belongs to a specific level and has: day(s) of the week, start time, end time, assigned professor, and maximum student limit per session. The system validates that the student limit is not exceeded during attendance registration. A class can be recurring (same days/times every week) or one-time. The manager can edit or deactivate an existing class. | P0 – Critical |
| RF-10 | Program – Cost Modification             | The administrator can modify the costs of the program modalities. **Acceptance Criteria:** Only the administrator (and the superadmin) can modify the cost of a program. The cost modification applies only to new memberships; it does not retroactively affect active ones. The system records a price change history with date, previous value, new value, and the user who made the change. | P1 – High   |

## 4.4 Student Management

| ID    | Module / Feature              | Description and Acceptance Criteria                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             | Priority    |
|-------|-------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------|
| RF-11 | Student – Complete Profile    | The system stores and manages the student's complete information. **Acceptance Criteria:** The student profile contains: first name, last name, date of birth, EPS, identity number, identity document type, and tutor data (if a minor). The student can edit their profile except for the identity number and document type (only the administrator can modify those). The administrator can search for students by name, last name, document number, or membership status. The system displays the age automatically calculated from the date of birth. | P0 – Critical |
| RF-12 | Student – Program Enrollment  | A student can be enrolled in a program and assigned to a level. **Acceptance Criteria:** The administrator or manager can enroll a student in a program by assigning them an initial level. A student can be enrolled in more than one program simultaneously. Upon enrollment, the student is placed in a 'no active membership' state until their payment is validated. | P0 – Critical |
| RF-13 | Student – Level Promotion     | A manager or professor can promote a student to a higher or lower level. **Acceptance Criteria:** The promotion requires selecting the target new level and optionally adding a justification note. The system records the student's level change history (date, source level, destination level, promoted by). Upon promotion, the student is assigned to the professor of the new level. The promotion does not affect the student's available hours. | P1 – High   |

## 4.5 Membership and Hour Management

| ID    | Module / Feature                          | Description and Acceptance Criteria                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             | Priority    |
|-------|-------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------|
| RF-14 | Membership – Creation and Activation      | Memberships are created by the administrator after validating the student's payment. **Acceptance Criteria:** A membership is associated with: student, program, number of purchased hours, start date (month of purchase), and expiration date (last day of the same calendar month). A membership cannot be activated without the payment having been validated by the administrator. If the administrator validates the payment, they can activate the membership directly or authorize the manager to do so. The system only allows one active membership per student per program at any given time. | P0 – Critical |
| RF-15 | Membership – Automatic Expiration         | Memberships expire automatically at the end of the calendar month in which they were acquired. **Acceptance Criteria:** The system evaluates daily (scheduled process) the status of all active memberships. A membership transitions to 'expired' status upon reaching the 1st day of the month following its activation. Unconsumed hours from an expired membership are not carried over to the following month; they are forfeited. The student receives a notification (email/in-app) at least 3 days before their membership expires. | P0 – Critical |
| RF-16 | Membership – Inactivation due to Depletion | A membership automatically becomes inactive when the student consumes all their hours. **Acceptance Criteria:** When the available hour balance reaches 0, the membership changes to 'inactive' status. With an inactive membership, the student cannot register attendance or access classes. The student and the manager receive a notification when the balance reaches 0. The system clearly displays the membership status on the student's dashboard. | P0 – Critical |
| RF-17 | Membership – Manual Hour Management       | The administrator can manually adjust a student's hour balance. **Acceptance Criteria:** The administrator can add hours (for additional payments or complimentary hours) or subtract hours (for error corrections). Each manual adjustment requires entering a justification reason and is recorded in the history. The adjustment cannot leave the balance negative; the system validates this. The manager does not have the ability to manually adjust hours; only the administrator and superadmin can. | P1 – High   |
| RF-18 | Membership – History and Traceability     | The system maintains a complete membership history per student. **Acceptance Criteria:** The history shows: purchase date, acquired hours, consumed hours, remaining hours, expiration date, and status for each membership. Each hour transaction is recorded: marked attendance (deduction), manual adjustment (addition/subtraction) with reason and responsible party. The administrator can export a student's history in CSV or PDF format. | P1 – High   |

## 4.6 Payment and Validation Flow

| ID    | Module / Feature                    | Description and Acceptance Criteria                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             | Priority    |
|-------|-------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------|
| RF-19 | Payment – Proof Upload              | The student can upload their payment proof from the platform. **Acceptance Criteria:** The student can upload a file (PDF, JPG, PNG) of up to 5 MB as proof of payment. The proof remains in 'pending validation' status until the administrator reviews it. The system notifies the administrator (email/in-app) when a proof is uploaded. The student can view the status of their proof: pending, approved, rejected. If rejected, the administrator must indicate the reason. | P0 – Critical |
| RF-20 | Payment – Administrator Validation  | The administrator validates or rejects the proof and activates the membership. **Acceptance Criteria:** The administrator accesses a queue of pending proofs ordered by upload date. Upon approving a proof, the system asks whether to activate the membership directly or to authorize the program manager. Upon rejection, the administrator enters a reason that is sent to the student via email and in-app notification. The validation history is recorded with: who validated, date and time, and outcome. | P0 – Critical |
| RF-21 | Payment – Manager Authorization     | When the administrator delegates, the manager receives authorization to activate the student's membership. **Acceptance Criteria:** The manager receives a notification indicating they are authorized to activate the membership of student X. The manager can activate the membership from their panel without requiring a new validation. If the manager does not activate the membership within 48 hours, the administrator receives a reminder. | P1 – High   |
| RF-22 | Payment – Payment History           | The administrator can view the complete payment history of all students. **Acceptance Criteria:** The history shows: student, program, upload date, validation date, status, amount (if applicable), and who validated. The history is filterable by student, program, status, and date range. The administrator can download the complete history in CSV format. | P1 – High   |

## 4.7 Attendance Registration and Cancellation (Student)

| ID    | Module / Feature                     | Description and Acceptance Criteria                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             | Priority    |
|-------|--------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------|
| RF-23 | Attendance – Student Registration    | The student can register their intent to attend a class by indicating the schedule and hours. **Acceptance Criteria:** The student can only register for classes in the program they are enrolled in and at their current level. Registration requires: class schedule selection and the number of hours they will attend. The system validates that the student has an active membership and sufficient available hours before allowing registration. The system validates that the class has not exceeded the student limit per session. Registration does not deduct hours at the time of registration; hours are deducted when the professor marks attendance. The student sees a visual confirmation of their successful registration. | P0 – Critical |
| RF-24 | Attendance – Student Cancellation    | The student can cancel their attendance registration for a class. **Acceptance Criteria:** The student can cancel their registration up to N hours before the class starts (time configurable by the administrator, default 2 hours). The cancellation releases the student's spot in that session. No hours are deducted if the cancellation is made within the permitted time. If the student cancels outside the permitted time, the system warns that hours may be deducted according to the league's policy (configurable). | P0 – Critical |

## 4.8 Attendance Marking (Professor / Manager)

| ID    | Module / Feature                          | Description and Acceptance Criteria                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             | Priority    |
|-------|-------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------|
| RF-25 | Attendance – Marking by Professor         | The professor marks the attendance of students who effectively show up to class. **Acceptance Criteria:** The professor sees the list of students registered for the current session of their class. They can mark each student as 'Present' or 'Absent'. Upon marking as 'Present', the system automatically deducts the corresponding hours from the student's membership. Attendance can only be marked for the current class (within a time window: from 30 minutes before until the end of the class). The system does not allow marking attendance for past sessions without manager authorization. If a present student has no available hours at the time of marking, the system alerts the professor but records the attendance with a 'no hours' status. | P0 – Critical |
| RF-26 | Attendance – Marking by Manager           | The manager can also mark attendance for any class in their program. **Acceptance Criteria:** The manager has access to the attendance marking view for any class within their program. The same hour deduction rules apply when the manager marks attendance. The manager can correct an erroneous marking within the following 24 hours (with a record of the correction). | P0 – Critical |
| RF-27 | Attendance – Class Alert by Professor     | The professor can change the status of a class session to "Alert" by attaching a reason. **Acceptance Criteria:** The professor can change the status of a future session or the current session before it starts. Changing the status to "Alert" mandatorily requires a text field with the reason (minimum 20 characters). Upon changing the status to "Alert", the system notifies all students registered in that session. The manager is notified when a professor changes the status of a class. | P1 – High   |
| RF-28 | Attendance – Class Cancellation by Professor | The professor can cancel a class session by attaching a reason. **Acceptance Criteria:** The professor can cancel a future session or the current session before it starts. Cancellation mandatorily requires a text field with the reason (minimum 20 characters). Upon cancellation, the system notifies all students registered in that session. The cancelled session does not deduct hours from any student. Students registered in the cancelled session automatically recover their spot. The manager is notified when a professor cancels a class. | P1 – High   |

## 4.9 Reports and Dashboards

| ID    | Module / Feature                        | Description and Acceptance Criteria                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             | Priority    |
|-------|-----------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------|
| RF-29 | Reports – Student Dashboard             | The student has a clear view of their current status on the platform. **Acceptance Criteria:** The dashboard displays: available hours, membership status, upcoming registered classes, and recent attendance history. The hour balance updates in real time after each attendance marking. The student can view their complete attendance history with date, class, and hours deducted. | P0 – Critical |
| RF-30 | Reports – Manager Dashboard             | The manager has operational visibility into their program. **Acceptance Criteria:** The dashboard displays: number of active students, students with available hours vs. without hours, today's classes, and marked vs. pending attendance. List of students with available hours filterable by level. Alert for students whose membership expires in the next 3 days. | P1 – High   |
| RF-31 | Reports – Administrator Dashboard       | The administrator has financial and operational visibility into the league. **Acceptance Criteria:** The dashboard displays: total active students, monthly revenue (based on validated payments), proofs pending validation, and memberships about to expire. Access to the complete payment history with filters. View of hour consumption by program and level. | P2 – Medium |
